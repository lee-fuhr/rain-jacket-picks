Best for specific needs

Best for hiking in heavy rain

For sustained downpours on the trail, protection and fit are king. The Arc’teryx Beta LT combines a proven Gore-Tex membrane with a trim but mobile cut that layers under a pack without bunching. Its hood adjusts cleanly over a helmet or hat, and the added breathability means you can keep hiking without soaking yourself from the inside out. Our pick: Arc’teryx Beta LT.

Best for everyday city use

When the rain hits during your commute or errands, you want something that does not look like a climbing shell but still keeps you dry. The Patagonia Torrentshell 3L offers a clean, low-key look and a cut that fits over a blazer or hoodie. It also has two hand pockets for your phone and keys, plus a solid warranty – ideal for day-to-day wear. Our pick: Patagonia Torrentshell 3L.

Best for ultralight travel

If every gram counts, the Outdoor Research Helium is almost unbeatable. It weighs less than most water bottles and folds into its own pocket, making it a no-brainer for backpackers and minimalist travelers. It breathes well enough for a light hiking pace, and the carabiner loop lets you clip it to the outside of your pack for quick access when the sky opens up. Our pick: Outdoor Research Helium Rain Jacket.

Our verdict

The right rain jacket depends on how you will use it, but if you only buy one, make it the Patagonia Torrentshell 3L – it is the most well-rounded performer for the money. For hikers who want to shave weight, grab the Outdoor Research Helium. Budget shoppers can count on the Columbia Watertight II. And if you are ready to invest in a premium shell that will handle years of alpine abuse, the Arc’teryx Beta LT is the gold standard. No matter which you choose, a good rain jacket is one purchase you will never regret when the clouds roll in.
